Donations & Gift Aid

View donations, manage Gift Aid declarations, and submit HMRC claims.

Viewing Donations

  1. Click Donations in the left sidebar under "Donations & Gift Aid".
  2. You'll see a list of all donations, showing the date, donor name, amount, and whether Gift Aid applies.
  3. Click on any donation to see the full details.
  4. Use the filters on the right to narrow by Gift Aid status, frequency (one-time/monthly), or payment source.
Donations from the website are recorded automatically when someone donates via the online donation page. You don't need to add them manually.

Understanding Gift Aid

Gift Aid allows the mosque to claim an extra 25% from HMRC on eligible donations at no cost to the donor. For example, a £100 donation means the mosque can claim £25 extra from HMRC.

How It Works

Step Who Does It What Happens
1 Donor Ticks "Gift Aid" when donating and provides their address
2 System Creates a Gift Aid Declaration automatically, linking the donor to their donations
3 You Create a Gift Aid Claim, add eligible donations, and submit to HMRC
4 HMRC Pays the 25% Gift Aid amount to the mosque

Gift Aid Declarations

A declaration is the donor's confirmation that they are a UK taxpayer and want Gift Aid to apply to their donations. These are created automatically when a donor opts in.

  1. Click Gift Aid Declarations in the sidebar to see all declarations.
  2. Each declaration shows the donor's name, reference number (e.g., GA-000001), and status.
  3. Active declarations are currently valid.
  4. Cancelled declarations are no longer in effect.

Creating a Gift Aid Claim (for HMRC)

You'll typically submit a Gift Aid claim to HMRC once a year (or quarterly). Here's how:

  1. Click Gift Aid Claims in the sidebar.
  2. Click "Add Gift Aid Claim".
  3. Enter a Reference (e.g., "GACLAIM-2026-001") and the claim period dates.
  4. In the Donations section, select all eligible donations for this period. Use the arrows to move donations between the "Available" and "Chosen" boxes.
  5. Click "Save". The totals will be calculated automatically.
  6. To export for HMRC, select the claim from the list, then use the Actions dropdown at the top:
    • Export as HMRC Gift Aid CSV — spreadsheet format for manual upload
    • Export as HMRC R68 XML — XML file for Charities Online portal
  7. Upload the exported file on the HMRC Charities Online portal.
  8. After submitting, update the claim Status to "Submitted" and record the Submitted Date.
The CSV format is easier if you're new to Gift Aid claims. The XML format is for the official HMRC Charities Online system.

Charity Gift Aid Settings

Before submitting your first claim, make sure your charity details are filled in:

  1. Click Charity Gift Aid Settings in the sidebar.
  2. Fill in your Charity Name and HMRC Reference (from your HMRC recognition letter).
  3. Add the Regulator details (Charity Commission number).
  4. Enter the Authorised Official — the person authorised to sign Gift Aid claims on behalf of the charity.
  5. Click "Save".
You can only have one set of Charity Gift Aid Settings. This is by design — they apply to the whole charity.

Generating Donation Receipts

  1. Go to the Donations list in the sidebar.
  2. Select the donation(s) you want to generate receipts for by ticking the checkbox on the left.
  3. From the Actions dropdown at the top, select "Generate donation receipt".
  4. Click "Go". The receipt will open in a new tab — you can print it or download it as a PDF.
