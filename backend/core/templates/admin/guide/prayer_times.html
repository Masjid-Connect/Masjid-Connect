Managing Prayer Times

Keep jama'ah times accurate so your community never misses a prayer.

Understanding Prayer Times

The app shows two types of prayer times to your community:

Type Where It Comes From What You Need to Do
Prayer Start Times Calculated automatically based on your mosque's location and calculation method Nothing — these update on their own
Jama'ah Times Set by your mosque — when the congregation actually prays You update these when they change
Prayer start times (when a prayer begins according to the sun's position) are calculated automatically. Jama'ah times (when your mosque actually holds the congregation prayer) are what you manage here.

How to Update Jama'ah Times

  1. Click Mosque Prayer Times in the left sidebar under "Mosques".
  2. You'll see a list of dates with jama'ah times. Find the date you want to update, or click "Add Mosque Prayer Time" for a new date.
  3. Select your Mosque and set the Date.
  4. Enter the jama'ah times for each prayer:
    • Fajr Jama'ah — e.g., 06:00
    • Dhuhr Jama'ah — e.g., 13:30
    • Asr Jama'ah — e.g., 16:45
    • Maghrib Jama'ah — usually shortly after sunset, e.g., 18:15
    • Isha Jama'ah — e.g., 20:00
  5. Click "Save".
Use 24-hour format for times (e.g., 13:30 instead of 1:30 PM). The app will display it in whichever format the user's phone prefers.

When to Update Times

Jama'ah times typically change:

  • Seasonally — as days get longer or shorter
  • During Ramadan — special Taraweeh times, adjusted Fajr
  • For Eid prayers — special one-off times
  • Clock changes — when daylight saving time starts or ends
If you don't update jama'ah times, the app will show the last times you entered. Community members rely on these being accurate — please update them whenever they change.
